SEPCO bill taxes explained

The taxes on a SEPCO bill are a stack of federal and provincial charges added on top of your energy cost: GST, electricity duty, the PTV licence fee, advance income tax under Section 235, and a late payment surcharge if you miss the due date. Each line has its own rule, and knowing those rules tells you which amounts you can reduce, claim back, or avoid entirely.

The tax stack, line by line

Every SEPCO bill starts with energy charges calculated by slab, then layers taxes and adjustments on top. One line sits in between: FPA, the fuel price adjustment. FPA is not a tax at all but a monthly pass-through that corrects the gap between the fuel cost NEPRA assumed in the tariff and what power generation actually cost. In months when fuel came in cheaper than assumed, FPA is negative and reduces your bill.

GST (general sales tax)

GST is the largest tax line for most households. It is charged as a percentage of the electricity value of the bill, so it rises automatically in a high-consumption month even when the rate itself has not changed. If your GST doubled, look first at your units, not at the tax rules.

Electricity duty

A small provincial duty that SEPCO collects on behalf of the Sindh government. It is a minor percentage of the energy charges and appears on virtually every bill. There is nothing to optimise here; it simply scales with usage.

PTV licence fee

A fixed fee collected through electricity bills to fund the state broadcaster. Domestic connections pay a small flat amount each month, while commercial connections pay a higher flat amount. Because it is fixed, it never changes with your units.

Advance income tax (Section 235)

Once a bill crosses the threshold set in the Income Tax Ordinance, advance income tax is withheld under Section 235. This is the one line many people can get back: if you are an active income tax filer, the withheld amount is adjustable against your annual liability when you file your return. Keep every monthly bill, or pull copies later using the steps in the duplicate bill guide, so you have proof of tax already paid.

Late payment surcharge (LPS)

LPS applies only when you pay after the due date, at roughly 10% of the bill for domestic connections. It is the most avoidable line on the bill; the due date and late payment guide covers exactly what happens as time passes.

Why is my tax so high?

When the tax block of a bill jumps, one of these is almost always the cause:

  • Your bill crossed the income tax threshold. Advance income tax only appears above a certain billed amount, so a single expensive month can add a line that was not there before.
  • The connection is billed as commercial. Commercial bills carry a higher PTV fee and different withholding treatment. Check the tariff category printed near the top of the bill; the bill reading guide shows where.
  • High units inflated the percentage taxes. GST and duty scale with consumption, and higher slabs raise the base they are calculated on. See what units mean and how charges are built.
  • A positive FPA month. The adjustment itself raises the base that some taxes apply to.
  • Last month's LPS became arrears. An unpaid surcharge rolls into the next bill and makes the whole total look heavier.

Filer tip: if you file income tax returns, the Section 235 advance tax on your electricity bills is not lost money. Save your bills through the year and hand them to whoever prepares your return, so the withheld amounts are adjusted against your final liability.

Is this page the official electricity company billing system?

No. This is an independent helper and guide site, not the official Sukkur Electric Power Company system. Always use official SEPCO channels for live billed amounts. The helper on this page only validates the 14 digit reference number format and builds a local estimate you can print for your own records.

The page is for people who need help with one practical billing task, such as finding a reference number, understanding taxes, printing a duplicate bill, or checking the bill on mobile. It is meant to answer that task clearly instead of staying broad and vague.

Can I still use the tool if I land directly on this supporting page?

Yes. The bill helper is available on every page, so you can validate a 14 digit reference number, estimate units, charges, and taxes, and print a summary without returning to the homepage first.

Why are there internal links to related bill pages here?
