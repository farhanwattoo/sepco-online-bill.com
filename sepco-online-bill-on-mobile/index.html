SEPCO online bill on mobile

Your phone can do the whole SEPCO bill routine — check the amount, save a PDF duplicate, share it on WhatsApp, and pay — in under two minutes, with nothing but the 14-digit reference number.

Checking the bill on Android

  1. Open Chrome and go to the official PITC bill portal (linked from SEPCO's official website).
  2. Enter the 14-digit reference number — digits only, no spaces.
  3. The bill loads with the amount, due date, and units. Zoom freely; the page is a normal web page.
  4. To keep a copy: tap the three-dot menu → Share → Print → choose Save as PDF.

Checking on iPhone

  1. Open Safari, load the portal, and enter the reference number the same way.
  2. Tap the Share button → Print → pinch out on the preview (or tap Save to Files) to store it as a PDF.

Slow connection tips

  • The portal is lightweight, but near the due date it gets crowded — early morning loads faster.
  • If the page half-loads on mobile data, retry on Wi-Fi or after clearing the tab; see common bill check errors for the full troubleshooting list.
  • Save the PDF once and you can re-open it offline all month.

Sharing and paying from the same phone

The saved PDF shares like any file — send it to a family member on WhatsApp, or to a print shop if you need paper (the printing guide covers shop printing). To pay, open your bank's app, JazzCash, or Easypaisa, choose electricity → SEPCO, and enter the same reference number; the current amount appears and you confirm. Channel-by-channel details are in the payment methods guide.

Make it a habit: save your reference number in your phone's notes or contacts, keep a "SEPCO bills" folder for the monthly PDFs, and set a reminder a few days after your usual meter-reading date. You will never chase a paper bill again.
