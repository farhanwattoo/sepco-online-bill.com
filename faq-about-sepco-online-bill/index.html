FAQ about SEPCO online bill

This is the question hub for SEPCO online bills. It maps out where every detailed guide lives — checking, downloading, understanding, paying, troubleshooting — and answers the odd questions that do not fit neatly into any of them.

How the guides on this site are organised

Every SEPCO billing question falls into one of five tasks, and each task has its own set of guides here.

Checking the bill. The core method is the 14-digit reference number on the PITC portal, covered step by step in check by reference number and, more broadly, in how to check a SEPCO bill online. If you keep hitting errors, common bill check errors lists every failure mode with its fix. Phone users should read checking on mobile.

Downloading and printing. When the paper copy is missing, start with the duplicate bill pillar guide, then follow how to download a duplicate bill for saving a PDF and how to print it so the copy is accepted at a bank counter.

Understanding the amounts. The line items split into two halves: charges (energy slabs, FPA, quarterly adjustment) and taxes (GST, duty, PTV fee, income tax). If the confusion starts earlier — what a "unit" even is — read units meaning.

Paying and timing. Every channel from bank counters to Easypaisa is compared in the payment methods guide, and what happens if you miss the deadline is covered in the due date and late payment guide.

Questions that do not fit the other guides

Does my reference number change every month?

No. The 14-digit reference number is fixed to your connection and stays the same on every monthly bill. It encodes your batch, sub-division, and account, so you can save it once — in your phone notes or as a contact entry — and never need the paper bill just to look up a new month.

Can someone else check my bill with my reference number?

Yes. The portal lookup has no password, so anyone holding the 14 digits can view the billed amount. That is convenient — a family member or shopkeeper can check or pay on your behalf — but it also means you should treat photos of your bill like mildly private documents, since they show your name, address, and consumption history.

Which areas does SEPCO actually cover?

SEPCO (Sukkur Electric Power Company) serves ten districts of upper Sindh: Sukkur, Ghotki, Khairpur, Shikarpur, Jacobabad, Kashmore-Kandhkot, Larkana, Qambar Shahdadkot, Dadu, and Naushahro Feroze. If your connection is outside these districts, your bill belongs to a different DISCO and a SEPCO reference number lookup will not find it, even though the national PITC system looks similar for every company.

Is this website the official SEPCO system?

No. This is an independent helper site. The tool above validates the reference number format and builds printable estimates for your own records; for the live billed amount, always use the official PITC bill portal reached through SEPCO's official website, or call the 118 helpline.
