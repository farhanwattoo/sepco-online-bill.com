How to Check Your SEPCO Bill Online

Checking a SEPCO bill takes less than a minute once you have your reference number. Here is the exact process, whether you use a computer or a phone.

Find your reference number

Pick up any previous SEPCO bill and copy the 14-digit reference number printed in the top section, near your name and address.

Open the bill portal

Go to the official PITC bill-check portal — the same system every Pakistani power company uses to serve bills online.

Enter the 14 digits

Type the reference number without spaces or dashes and submit. Your latest bill appears with the amount and due date.

Download or print

Use your browser's print or save-as-PDF option to keep a duplicate copy — banks and payment shops accept this printout.

What Is the SEPCO Online Bill?

SEPCO — the Sukkur Electric Power Company — is the electricity distribution company for upper Sindh. Every month it issues a paper bill to more than a million connections, and the same bill is published online through the national PITC billing system. The online copy is what people call the SEPCO online bill: it shows your units consumed, energy charges, taxes, due date, and payment history, and it can be downloaded, saved as a PDF, or printed as a duplicate bill that is accepted everywhere the original is.

Checking the bill online matters for a simple reason: paper bills get lost, arrive late, or never arrive at all — and the due date does not wait. With the 14-digit reference number you can always see exactly what you owe and print a copy in time to pay without a late payment surcharge.

Check by 14-Digit Reference Number

The reference number is the key to everything. It is a 14-digit code printed in the top section of every SEPCO bill, and it stays the same month after month for the same connection. The digits encode your batch, sub-division, and account, which is how the portal finds your bill instantly.

Lost every copy of your bill? Ask a neighbour on the same street for their bill — the first digits identify your area's batch and sub-division — then confirm your full number at the local SEPCO office or through the 118 helpline.

Download & Print a Duplicate Bill

A duplicate bill is simply a reprint of the current month's bill. Once the bill is on screen you can print it directly or save it as a PDF on your phone and print later. Banks, post offices, and mobile-wallet agents all accept a duplicate printout for payment. If you only need the amount and reference number, many bank counters can also pull up your bill from the reference number alone.

Understand the Charges on Your Bill

A SEPCO bill is more than units × rate. The total is built from energy charges plus a stack of adjustments, taxes, and fees. Here are the line items you will usually see:

Line itemWhat it is
Energy chargesUnits consumed billed by tariff slab — the more units, the higher the per-unit rate.
FPAFuel price adjustment — a monthly correction for fuel costs; it can be positive or negative.
Quarterly adjustmentPeriodic tariff correction approved by NEPRA, applied to eligible consumers.
GSTGeneral sales tax applied to the electricity value of the bill.
Electricity dutyA small provincial duty calculated on energy charges.
PTV feeFixed television licence fee collected with the bill (higher for commercial connections).
Income taxAdvance income tax on bills above set thresholds; adjustable for tax filers.
LPSLate payment surcharge, added only if you pay after the due date.

Where to Pay a SEPCO Bill

Once you know the amount, you can pay through any of these channels — always keep the receipt or transaction ID until the payment shows on next month's bill:

  • Bank counters and post offices — hand over the bill (or duplicate printout) and pay cash.
  • ATM and mobile banking — most Pakistani banks list SEPCO under utility bill payments; enter the reference number.
  • JazzCash and Easypaisa — pay from the app or at any agent shop with the reference number.
  • Internet banking — add SEPCO as a biller once, then pay each month in a few taps.

See the full comparison in the SEPCO bill payment methods guide, and what the due date really means in the due date and late payment guide.

Missed the due date? Don't wait for a disconnection notice. The bill's "payable after due date" amount already includes the surcharge — pay that amount at any channel above, then check next month's bill to confirm the arrears cleared.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions SEPCO consumers ask most about checking, downloading, and paying their bill.

How do I check my SEPCO bill online?

Take the 14-digit reference number printed on any previous SEPCO bill and enter it in the official PITC bill portal to see the live billed amount. You can also use the helper on this page to validate the number format and prepare a printable estimate first.

Where is the 14-digit reference number printed on a SEPCO bill?

It appears in the top section of every printed SEPCO bill, usually in a boxed row near your name and address. Any older bill for the same connection carries the same reference number.

Can I check my SEPCO bill by CNIC, name, or meter number?

No. The online lookup works only with the 14-digit reference number or the customer ID. CNIC, name, and meter number are not accepted by the bill portal, but you can recover your reference number from an old bill, a neighbour's bill plus your account details, or your local SEPCO office.

What is the difference between the reference number and the customer ID?

The 14-digit reference number identifies your connection for bill lookup and encodes your batch, sub-division, and account. The 10-digit customer ID is a separate account identifier used on some payment channels. Both are printed on the bill.

How do I download a SEPCO duplicate bill?

Open the official bill portal, enter your 14-digit reference number, and use your browser's print or save-as-PDF option to download the full duplicate bill. Our duplicate bill guide walks through every step with tips for mobile phones.

Which areas does SEPCO cover?

Sukkur Electric Power Company serves upper Sindh, including Sukkur, Ghotki, Khairpur, Shikarpur, Jacobabad, Kashmore-Kandhkot, Larkana, Qambar Shahdadkot, Dadu, and Naushahro Feroze.

Does this website show live SEPCO bill data?

No. This is an independent helper site. The tool validates the reference number format and builds a local estimate only. For the officially billed amount, always use the official SEPCO or PITC channels.

What taxes and charges appear on a SEPCO bill?

A typical SEPCO bill includes energy charges by slab, fuel price adjustment (FPA), quarterly tariff adjustments, general sales tax, electricity duty, the PTV licence fee, income tax where applicable, and a late payment surcharge if you pay after the due date.

What happens if I pay my SEPCO bill after the due date?

A late payment surcharge is added, typically around ten percent for domestic connections. The bill shows both amounts: payable within due date and payable after due date. Repeated non-payment can eventually lead to disconnection, so pay the after-due-date amount as soon as possible.

What should I do if my SEPCO bill never arrived?

You still have to pay by the due date. Get a duplicate bill using your 14-digit reference number on the official portal, or visit your sub-divisional SEPCO office. Our bill-not-received guide covers the exact steps.
