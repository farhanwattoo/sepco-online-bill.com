SEPCO Online Bill Check

You can check any Sukkur Electric Power Company (SEPCO) bill online in under a minute. All you need is the 14-digit reference number printed on any previous bill — the official portal run by PITC shows the current amount, due date, and a printable copy free of charge.

Check your actual bill: the official SEPCO bill portal is bill.pitc.com.pk/sepcobill. Enter your 14-digit reference number to view, download, or print the real bill free of charge.

Quick steps

  1. Find the 14-digit reference number on any old SEPCO bill (top-left area of the printed bill).
  2. Open the official portal: bill.pitc.com.pk/sepcobill.
  3. Type the reference number without spaces or dashes and submit.
  4. Your bill appears on screen — check the payable amount within due date and the higher amount that applies after the due date.
  5. Use your browser's print or save-as-PDF option if you need a copy for payment at a bank.

No printer? No problem

Most bank branches, JazzCash and Easypaisa agents, and NADRA e-Sahulat shops only need the reference number to accept payment — you usually don't need the paper bill at all. If a branch insists on a printed copy, see our printing guide.

If the portal says no record was found or rejects your number, our error troubleshooting guide covers every common cause and fix.

Frequently asked questions

Is checking a SEPCO bill online free?

Yes. The official PITC portal is completely free with no registration or login. You only need the 14-digit reference number.

Can I check someone else's bill?

Yes — the portal only asks for a reference number, so you can check a bill for a family member, tenant, or landlord if you have their number.

How early does the new bill appear online?

The online bill is usually available a few days after meter reading, around the same time the paper bill is printed — typically in the first half of the month for most SEPCO subdivisions.
